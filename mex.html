~ About My Contribution to the ETSI TETRA Standard ~
What is the Multimedia Exchange Layer? I wrote Clause 7.2 – Multimedia Exchange (MEX) Layer of the ETSI TETRA standard, which defines how packet data communication can be performed while meeting the required Quality of Service (QoS) in TETRA systems. Packet data may include voice, video, still images as well as data from a plethora of IoT sensors, and the available radio network resources need to be allocated efficiently to transmit mixed packet data in real time. The MEX layer provides an efficient time division multiple access mechanism that enables the transmission of mixed (multimedia) data using the wireless TETRA networks. My contribution involved extensive research and collaboration with industry experts to ensure that the specifications met the needs of public safety and mission-critical communication users. The Multimedia Exchange Layer is crucial for enabling advanced communication capabilities in TETRA systems, enhancing interoperability and performance across different devices and applications.
