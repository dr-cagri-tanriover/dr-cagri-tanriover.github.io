~ Cagri Tanriover's Education ~
Doctoral Degree (PhD), Lancaster University – United Kingdom – [October 1998, February 2002]
  • Synopsis: I received my PhD in Communications Systems and my research was in Turbo Codes, which are a class of channel capacity–approaching error correction codes. My focus was on improving the architecture of this class of codes, and to demonstrate how architectural enhancements yielded practical improvements in applications such as compressed still image transmission over noisy wireless channels. As the key output of my research, I invented the new "Multifold Turbo Coding" technique – in collaboration with Dr Shu Lin and his team – that improved the bit and frame error rate of the state-of-the-art turbo codes. I also published several peer-reviewed journal and conference papers as listed below.

Masters Degree (MSc), Lancaster University – United Kingdom – [October 1997, May 1998]
  • Synopsis: I received my MSc in "Digital Signal Processing Applications in Communication Systems", which I completed with distinction. My thesis was on the exploration of how quantization affected the bit error rate of Turbo codes – a class of channel capacity–approaching error correction codes invented in 1993. Besides providing a quantitative analysis of the quantization effects, I also simulated still image transmission over noisy wireless channels to demonstrate the visual impact of channel errors and quantization. My MSc work laid the foundation for my later PhD research in the same field.

Bachelor of Science Degree (BSc), Istanbul Technical University (ITU) – Turkey – [October 1993, June 1997]
  • Synopsis: I received my BSc in "Electronics and Telecommunications Engineering" in Turkey. During my final year, I completed a capstone project focused on the design and implementation of digital filters using an ordinary microcontroller rather than a digital signal processor, and studied the limitations of this approach.
International Baccalaureate (IB), United World College USA (UWC-USA) – NM, USA – [August 1990, May 1992]
  • Synopsis: I have an IB diploma from UWC-USA, which is one of the 18 international schools worldwide that are part of the United World Colleges movement. Each UWC school accepts 100 students per year, and each student has to be approved by his/her country's national committee to qualify. UWC-USA is known for its rigorous academic curriculum and its emphasis on intercultural understanding, community service, and leadership development. My time at UWC-USA was transformative, as it exposed me to a diverse community of students from around the world and shaped me into the person I am today.
